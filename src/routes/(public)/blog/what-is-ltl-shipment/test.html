What is LTL shipping?

Mario Sanchez

October 21, 2022· 8 min read

Introduction

LTL freight is a cost-effective way to ship pallets. LTL refers to “less than truckload” freight, which means that you'll be shipping pallets in a trailer with other items.

LTL freight is one of the most popular options for small and medium-sized businesses who ship frequently but don't have the resources of larger corporations. The benefits of using an LTL carrier include lower prices compared to full truckload (FTL) shipments, increased flexibility in destinations and transit times, and reduced transportation costs per item shipped by sharing space with other customers' products on a truck or trailer headed in the same direction as yours. The disadvantages of using an LTL carrier are that you won't always have control over the exact route taken to get your freight from point A to point B, and there's a greater chance that it will be damaged in transit. Some companies also offer TL services, which means they'll send a truck out with just your cargo on board—which is usually cheaper than paying for all the space in a trailer (especially if you're shipping one or two pallets).

Understanding LTL Shipping

Freight shipping is an important part of any company’s supply chain. It can be a great source of revenue, but it also comes with some risk. That’s why it’s essential that you understand what LTL freight is and how it works before you commit to sending your products on the road.

Here are some key points about LTL freight:

Freight shipping involves transporting goods in a truck or trailer from one location to another. The difference between LTL freight and truckload (or TL) is that LTL uses smaller vehicles — trucks as small as 26 feet — while TL uses bigger ones ranging up to 53 feet long. This allows companies like FEDEX Ground, UPS Freight, or FedEx Freight to use their existing equipment when moving smaller amounts of goods across short distances within one area or city rather than transporting them between far-flung regions by rail or plane.

How LTL Freight Works

LTL freight is a flexible, cost-effective way to ship pallets. It's a great option for businesses that have large quantities of materials that are not time sensitive. LTL freight is also ideal for companies with high inventory needs, as it allows them to ship in small batches and have their products delivered quickly.
